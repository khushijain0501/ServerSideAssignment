Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a revered mosque and tomb located on an islet off the coast of Worli in Mumbai. It's a significant pilgrimage site for Muslims and a landmark attracting people of all faiths. The dargah houses the tomb of Sayyed Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a wealthy 15th-century merchant from Bukhara (present-day Uzbekistan). According to legend, he abandoned his worldly possessions and undertook a pilgrimage to Mecca before settling in Mumbai. The dargah was built in the early 19th century. Haji Ali Dargah is known for its exquisite Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring white domes and minarets. The dargah complex also includes a mosque and facilities for pilgrims. Visitors can pay respects at the tomb, seek blessings, and experience the serene atmosphere. It's an important cultural and spiritual landmark in Mumbai.
