Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves, also known as Gharapuri Caves, are a group of rock-cut cave temples located on Elephanta Island, Mumbai Harbour. They are primarily dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva and are considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The caves were carved between the 6th and 8th centuries AD. The most prominent sculpture in the caves is the Trimurti (three-headed) Sadashiva, depicting the three forms of Shiva: the Creator, the Preserver, and the Destroyer. Other notable sculptures include the Ardhanarishwara (half-male, half-female form of Shiva), and various panels depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. The Elephanta Caves are a significant example of Indian rock-cut architecture and cave art. They offer a glimpse into the artistic and religious traditions of the era.
