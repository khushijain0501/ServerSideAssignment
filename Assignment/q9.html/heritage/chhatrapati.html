Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is a historic railway station and UNESCO World Heritage Site in Mumbai. It's renowned for its Victorian Gothic Revival architecture, featuring elaborate carvings, pointed arches, and stained-glass windows. Construction began in 1878 and was completed in 1887. The station was designed by the British architect F. W. Stevens and is considered a masterpiece of Victorian Gothic architecture. The station is named after the Maratha emperor Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus is not only a transportation hub but also a major tourist attraction. It's a testament to Mumbai's colonial past and architectural heritage.
