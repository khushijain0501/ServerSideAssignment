Afghan Church

The Afghan Church, also known as the Church of St John the Evangelist, isn't actually a church for Afghan people or related to Afghanistan in the religious sense. It's a historical Anglican church in Mumbai, India, built as a war memorial. Built between 1847 and 1858, the church commemorates the British soldiers who died in the First Anglo-Afghan War (1838-1840) and the disastrous retreat from Kabul.Beyond its religious function, the Afghan Church is a significant landmark in Mumbai's architectural and cultural heritage. It also serves as a community hub by hosting concerts, exhibitions, and free clinics for students.
